# ROUND 1 FINDINGS: THE ±3° EMPIRICAL VERIFICATION

## Executive Summary

The ±3° claim does not hold as a precise cross-domain constant. Of 14 claims checked across four domains, zero were fully confirmed at exactly ±3°, two were outright falsified, and the remainder showed values that scatter across a range from ±0.5° to ±4°, with most clustering below the claimed ±3°. The honest assessment is that ±3° functions as a rough order-of-magnitude anchor -- human sensorimotor and perceptual systems do operate within single-digit-degree tolerance bands -- but the specific value of 3 is not privileged by the evidence. Several cited sources were misattributed or do not contain the figures claimed.

## Domain-by-Domain Results

### 1. Biomechanical Tolerance Bands and Oscillatory Modes

**DEF-01b: Postural Sway ±3° Cone**
- **Claimed:** Center-of-pressure oscillation within ±3° cone during quiet standing (Winter 1995, Peterka 2002).
- **Actual evidence:** Typical body sway is ±0.5° or less. CoM RMS displacement is 2-5mm in the AP direction; at a CoM height of ~1m, this yields ~0.57°. The cited sources report data in millimeters, not degrees.
- **Verdict:** FALSIFIED
- **Key surprise:** The claim overstates the actual value by roughly 6x. Winter 1995 and Peterka 2002 do not report a ±3° figure. This is a significant misattribution.

**DEF-01d: Subtalar Joint ±3° Pronation/Supination**
- **Claimed:** ±3° pronation/supination from neutral during gait (Root, Orien & Weed 1977, Kirby 2001).
- **Actual evidence:** Peak pronation reaches 3-4° from neutral (partially matching), but motion is asymmetric. Supination during late stance reaches ~6° from neutral. Total available supination ROM is 8-12°.
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- **Key surprise:** The ±3° symmetric framing obscures the fundamentally asymmetric nature of subtalar motion during gait (roughly 2° supination to 4° pronation).

**DEF-01g: Throwing Accuracy ±3° Deviation Cone**
- **Claimed:** ±3° deviation cone for on-target delivery (Hore, Watts & Tweed 1996).
- **Actual evidence:** Hore 1996 found that throwing errors arise from release timing, not angular trajectory deviations. Skilled baseball pitchers show release angle SD of ~0.7-1.1°. Full text of Hore 1996 was not accessible.
- **Verdict:** UNDERDETERMINED
- **Key surprise:** The cited paper actually undermines the angular-deviation-cone framing entirely, finding timing (milliseconds) rather than geometry (degrees) as the error mechanism.

**DEF-08: Oscillatory Frequency Ranges**
- **Claimed:** Postural sway 0.1-0.5 Hz, Respiratory rhythm 0.3-1.0 Hz, Gait cadence 1.5-3.0 Hz, Cardiac rhythm 1.0-3.0 Hz.
- **Actual evidence:** Postural sway range is correct. The other three have reasonable lower bounds but significantly inflated upper bounds (respiratory 1.0 Hz = 60 breaths/min is pathological tachypnea; gait 3.0 Hz is running; cardiac 3.0 Hz = 180 bpm is near-maximal exercise).
- **Verdict:** REFINED
- **Key surprise:** The ranges appear designed to span rest-to-maximum-exertion, but are presented as characteristic rhythm frequencies, which is misleading.

### 2. Rotational Athletics and Physics

**DEF-01e: Golf Clubface ±3° for Straight Flight**
- **Claimed:** ±3° functional accuracy window for straight ball flight (Nesbit & Serrano 2005).
- **Actual evidence:** ±3° produces a definite fade or draw, not straight flight. Straight flight requires ±1-1.5°. PGA pros control face angle within ~1°. ±3° is the boundary between playable and problematic, not the window for straight flight.
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- **Key surprise:** Nesbit & Serrano 2005 is misattributed -- their paper addressed work and energy analysis of the golf swing, not clubface angle tolerance.

**DEF-01f: Baseball Bat-Ball Contact ±3°**
- **Claimed:** Vertical and horizontal precision window of ±3° (Nathan 2003, Cross 2011).
- **Actual evidence:** The hitting physics literature does not frame precision as a single angular window. It uses timing error (2.48-30.40 ms), spatial offset (millimeters), and attack angle matching (degrees relative to pitch descent) as separate dimensions.
- **Verdict:** UNDERDETERMINED
- **Key surprise:** The claim collapses a multi-dimensional precision problem into a single number that neither cited author actually published.

**DEF-05-RAINBOW: 42° = Critical Angle of Total Internal Reflection**
- **Claimed:** 42° corresponds to the critical angle of total internal reflection in water.
- **Actual evidence:** 42° is the primary rainbow observation angle (confirmed robustly: 42.08° for n=1.333). But it results from minimum deviation, not total internal reflection. The TIR critical angle for water is ~48.75°.
- **Verdict:** REFINED
- **Key surprise:** The 42° number is correct; the physical mechanism cited is wrong. TIR and minimum deviation are fundamentally different phenomena.

**DEF-05-XFACTOR: 42° = Peak Elastic Loading Angle**
- **Claimed:** 42° corresponds to the empirically observed peak elastic loading angle of the human trunk (X-factor).
- **Actual evidence:** TPI data reports tour averages of 42-45°; McLean 1992 found 38° for long hitters. The range across studies is 38-50°. "Peak elastic loading angle" is not standard terminology; research shows X-Factor Stretch (dynamic increase during transition) matters more than the static value.
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- **Key surprise:** 42° falls within the range but is not a universal biomechanical constant. The framing as a single optimal value overstates what is actually a 38-50° spread.

### 3. Classical Architecture and Optics

**DEF-01c: Column Entasis ±3° Deviation**
- **Claimed:** Profile deviation from true cylinder is approximately ±3° in Greek Doric and Ionic orders.
- **Actual evidence:** The Parthenon entasis deviation is ~0.21° (17.5mm over a 9.56m shaft). The overall taper half-angle is ~0.95°. Archaeological literature uses ratios (1:546) or millimeters, never angular degrees.
- **Verdict:** FALSIFIED
- **Key surprise:** The claim is off by more than an order of magnitude. The actual entasis deviation is roughly 15x smaller than ±3°. A 3° taper would reduce the column top to 47% of its base diameter -- visually absurd for any known classical column.

**DEF-01c-context: Entasis as Optical Correction**
- **Claimed:** Entasis functions as an optical correction, and ±3° is standard in archaeological literature.
- **Actual evidence:** The optical correction interpretation is longstanding (Vitruvius) but actively debated. The ±3° figure does not appear in any archaeological source found.
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (for optical correction function); FALSIFIED (for ±3° figure)

**DEF-05-RAINBOW (Architecture domain check): 42° = Primary Rainbow Angle**
- **Confirmed:** 42.08° for yellow-green light (n=1.333), ranging from 40.6° (violet) to 42.3° (red). Robust first-principles derivation dating to Descartes 1637.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED

**RP3-P2: Fractal Dimension of Entasis Profiles**
- **Claimed:** Fractal dimension of entasis profiles approximates d*.
- **Actual evidence:** No published study has computed the fractal dimension of an entasis profile. Since entasis curves are smooth (parabolic or conic), their fractal dimension is trivially 1.0.
- **Verdict:** UNDERDETERMINED (no evidence exists; the claim is speculative)

### 4. Flow States, Calendar Deviation, Neural Fractals, and Economic Tolerance

**DEF-01h: Flow State ±3% Challenge-Skill Boundary**
- **Claimed:** Optimal engagement at ±3% deviation from current capacity (Csikszentmihalyi 1990, Nakamura & Csikszentmihalyi 2002).
- **Actual evidence:** Neither cited source contains any percentage figure. The only published number is Kotler's "4% above" rule (from "Rise of Superman," 2014), which Kotler himself described as originating from a "sketchy, back-of-the-envelope calculation." The actual value is 4% above (unidirectional), not ±3% (symmetric).
- **Verdict:** REFINED
- **Key surprise:** Double misattribution -- wrong sources cited, and the number was changed from 4% unidirectional to ±3% symmetric.

**DEF-01i: Calendar Deviation ±3 Days**
- **Claimed:** Monthly deviation from 30.44-day mean is approximately ±3 days.
- **Actual evidence:** Maximum deviation is 2.44 days (February). 11 of 12 months deviate by less than 0.6 days. The distribution is extremely asymmetric, not a ±3 day band.
- **Verdict:** REFINED
- **Key surprise:** The claim overstates the maximum by 23% and completely misrepresents the distribution. The pattern is one outlier (February at -2.44 days) with everything else clustered within ±0.6 days.

**RP3-P5: Neural Fractal Dimension d* ~ 1.5**
- **Claimed:** Healthy waking cognition and flow states cluster at d* ≈ 1.5.
- **Actual evidence:** Depends entirely on which fractal measure is used. Higuchi FD can include values near 1.5 for some parameters. Cortical connectivity FD for healthy controls is ~1.73. DFA exponents are 0.5-1.0 (with 1.5 being Brownian noise, not healthy cognition). No study has measured any fractal dimension during flow states.
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- **Key surprise:** The value 1.5 in DFA specifically represents Brownian noise (less complex), not healthy cognition. The flow-state portion is entirely unsupported by any published measurement.

**RP3-P4: Economic Tolerance Bands ±3° Equivalent**
- **Claimed:** Economic tolerance bands approximate ±3° equivalent.
- **Actual evidence:** Central bank inflation targets typically use ±1-2 percentage points around a 2-3% target. The most common band is ±1 percentage point. Emerging markets use wider bands (±1.5-2pp).
- **Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- **Key surprise:** The actual bands are closer to ±1-2 percentage points, not ±3. The degree-to-percentage-point mapping is a loose metaphor, not a rigorous equivalence.

## The Convergence Question

**How many domains genuinely show ±3°?**

Zero domains show ±3° as a precise, well-sourced empirical value. The closest results are:
- Subtalar pronation peaks at 3-4° from neutral (one side of an asymmetric range)
- Golf clubface ±3° marks the playable/problematic boundary (but not straight flight)
- X-factor averages include 42° in one TPI report (but the range is 38-50°)

**How many show a nearby but different value?**

Several domains show tolerance bands in the low single digits, but at different values:
- Postural sway: ±0.5° (6x smaller than claimed)
- Column entasis: ±0.21° (15x smaller than claimed)
- Throwing accuracy: ±0.7-1.1° SD for skilled throwers
- Straight golf flight: ±1-1.5°
- Calendar deviation: max 2.44 days (not 3)
- Inflation targets: ±1-2 percentage points (not 3)

**Is the dimensional equivalence rigorous?**

No. The framework maps degrees, percentages, days, and fractal dimensions onto a single "±3" template. These are fundamentally different quantities:
- Angular degrees (spatial geometry)
- Percentages of capacity (psychological scaling)
- Calendar days (temporal arithmetic)
- Fractal dimensions (mathematical topology)

There is no physical or mathematical basis for treating "3 degrees," "3 percent," and "3 days" as equivalent expressions of the same underlying constant. The mapping is metaphorical, not rigorous.

**Assessment:** The pattern is better described as "human sensorimotor systems operate within low-single-digit-degree tolerance bands" -- a true but unsurprising observation. The convergence on exactly 3 appears to result from selection bias (choosing examples that approximate 3 and rounding toward it) rather than from a genuine cross-domain constant.

## Implications for the Framework

**If the actual convergence is ±1-2° rather than ±3°:**

- **The 2D = 6° tolerance band** becomes 2D = 2-4°, which changes the geometric relationships in the compiler. The 6° band is likely too wide by a factor of 2-3x for the domains where angular tolerances are best measured (postural sway, throwing accuracy, straight golf flight).

- **The B = 42° angular budget** is the most robust number in the framework. The primary rainbow angle is genuinely 42° from first principles. The X-factor falls in the 38-50° range with 42° as one reported average. If D is closer to 1-1.5° rather than 3°, then B/D = 42/1.5 = 28, not 42/3 = 14. The ratio changes significantly.

- **The compiler architecture** needs to distinguish between:
  1. Robustly confirmed constants (42° rainbow angle)
  2. Order-of-magnitude estimates (sensorimotor tolerance is low single digits of degrees)
  3. Unsupported claims (neural fractal d* during flow states, entasis fractal dimension)

The architecture should not depend on ±3° being exact. If it is parameterized so that D is a tunable value rather than a hardcoded constant, the framework becomes more honest and more useful.

## Open Questions for Round 2+

1. **Source verification pattern:** Multiple claims had misattributed citations (Nesbit & Serrano 2005, Csikszentmihalyi 1990 for percentage, Winter 1995 for angular degrees). Systematic source-checking needed.
2. **What value does the evidence converge on?** Most verified tolerances fall in ±0.5-2°, not ±3°.
3. **Is 42° genuinely multi-domain?** Rainbow angle is physically rigorous. X-factor is variable (38-50°). Other 42° domains?
4. **Flow state fractal dimension:** No published measurement exists. Flagged as testable hypothesis.
5. **The asymmetry problem:** Multiple domains show asymmetric tolerance bands. The ±D framing assumes symmetry.
6. **Mechanism vs. magnitude:** Throwing errors are timing-based, not angular. In how many domains is the angular framing itself the wrong dimension?
7. **Falsifiability criterion:** At what point does the framework update its core constant vs. treating discrepancy as noise?
